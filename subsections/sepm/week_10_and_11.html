Week 10 & 11


Reading

The core reading of week 10 revolved around software quality. I was introduced to the book Software Engineering at Google : Lessons Learned from Programming over Time which I found to be a superb book. A portion of the value of science itself is composed of the value of derivative engineering. So having the opportunity to learn from a book written by engineers of one of the most culturally integrated softwares in existence was fascinating. I was delighted by the genereous use of analogies in the book to complement key ideas, such as the link between Hyrum's Law of API usage and thermodynamic entropy, revealing inspiring insights into technical communication; alongside the topical content about software engineering and quality. This is a book I will be revisiting in the future.

Week 11 was about future project management research directions, and the one that interested me the most is to do with knowledge management. This is currently by preferred option as a thesis topic.
